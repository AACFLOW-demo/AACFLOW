Acoustic Augmented Conditioning Music Generation with Flow Matching Transformer

Abstract

Text to music (TTM) generation models typically generate music signals solely conditioned on textual input. While effective for semantic alignment, this traditional approach often struggles to capture detailed acoustic features inherent in music signal, e.g., prosody, timbre and expressiveness etc, leading to outputs that may sound unnatural or lack desired sonic qualities. To address this limitation, we propose a novel hybrid conditioning paradigm. Our method leverages MERT, a self-supervised large scale pretrained acoustic music foundation model, to extract high-level and generalizable acoustic features from a music signal. These extracted music features are then fused with the textual description to form an acoustic augmented condition (AAC) for the generative network, which is a flow-matching transformer architecture. Thus, the proposed approach, denoted as AACFlow, with piecewise linear dropout training strategy and classifier-free guidance (CFG)-Zero* inference strategy, significantly enriches the conditioning information by incorporating the acoustic prior. Experimental results show that AACFlow substantially improve the controllability and quality of the generated music signals compared to the conventional text-only conditioning methods on major benchmarks.
